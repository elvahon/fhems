About FHEMS

Fredie Hung Ethnic Minorities Service (FHEMS) is initiated in Spring 2020 to connect and allocate relevant social resources and community networks, to enhance accessibility to information and knowledge of civil society, to magnify their participation in Hong Kong as citizens, and to promote inter-cultural mutual understandings. Works have been done to fulfill both the needs of anti-pandemic materials and also information, such as continuous masks distribution, and broadcasting social distancing measures and pandemic situation updates.

Who is Fredie

I’m Fredie Hung, registered social worker in HK, graduate of master in anthropology and religious studies in CUHK. After graduation, I have been living in Athens and Mumbai for one year each to study Greek and Sanskrit respectively. As a beginner-learner of Nepalese, Hindi, Arabic and Khmer, it is my pleasure to learn more from different ethnic communities in HK. Please share your language and culture with me. I’ve been volunteering in HK Anatolia Cultural Centre in organizing Cantonese lessons for Turkish who stays in HK. Currently, I’m the advisor of HK Integrated Nepalese Society and the founding member of WorKhmer, an NGO concerning human trafficking in Cambodia.
